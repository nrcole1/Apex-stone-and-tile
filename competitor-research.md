## Tier 1 — Direct competitors to watch

### 1. Sand & Stone Tile — your closest analog
- **Site:** https://sandandstonetile.com/ · Serves the *identical* footprint (Nassau County, Yulee, Fernandina, Amelia).
- **How the site works:** Modern local-SEO build with per-city + per-service landing pages and clear "free, no-obligation estimate" CTAs. **Publishes a price range ($8–$25/sq ft)** — rare and trust-building.
- **Reviews:** Almost no visible review footprint (on Houzz, but no star count surfaced). **This is the opening:** the closest tile/stone name to yours has thin social proof — out-reviewing them is very achievable.
- **Copy:** Per-city/service pages; transparent pricing band.

### 2. Noble Granite — the countertop heavyweight
- **Site:** https://ngranite.com (+ noblegranite.net). Jacksonville, since 2006, claims 20,000+ jobs.
- **How the site works:** Best-in-market **per-city/county landing pages** (dedicated Fernandina Beach, Amelia Island, Yulee, Nassau County URLs), a Portfolio page, and a dedicated Reviews page. Free-estimate CTA.
- **Reviews:** **4.9 / ≈274 (Birdeye)**, 4.8 Angi, 5.0 Houzz. Strong. BBB: not accredited.
- **Copy:** The city-page matrix + aggregated reviews page.

### 3. Tri-City Stone — the on-island countertop rival
- **Site:** https://tri-citystone.com + a geo-microsite https://tricitystonefernandinabeach.com. Showroom at 1721 S 8th St, Fernandina Beach.
- **How the site works:** Main site + Fernandina microsite, plus a **blog** for SEO. Leans on "family / woman / veteran-owned, on the island" identity.
- **Reviews:** Inconsistent — **Yelp 3.0 from just 2 reviews** (claims ~4.8 across 200+ elsewhere). Low verified volume = another review opening for you.
- **Copy:** Local-identity branding; geo-targeted microsite.

## Tier 2 — Strong, adjacent (flooring-led)

### 4. American Flooring of Yulee — the review king
- **Site:** https://www.americanflooringyulee.com/ — pro site with Installation, **Reviews**, and product pages; Mohawk dealer, since 2003.
- **Reviews:** **4.9 with ≈270–315 (Birdeye)** — by far the largest review base in the area; 4.9 HomeAdvisor. Their review machine is the #1 trust advantage in this market.
- **Copy:** Relentless review generation + a dedicated on-site Reviews page.

### 5. Florida Mud Masters — the content marketer
- **Site:** https://floridamudmasters.com/ — deep per-city pages, an **active blog**, and **live Google reviews embedded** (Trustindex widget).
- **Copy:** Embedded live-review widget + blog. Only competitor doing real content marketing.

### 6. Dan's Floor Store — trust via awards
- **Site:** https://dansfloorstoreinc.com/ — dedicated tile/stone and wood landing pages, a **testimonials page**, "free in-home consultation" CTA, showroom.
- **Reviews:** 5.0 Houzz, 4.8 Birdeye; displays "Best of the Best" award badges.
- **Copy:** Award/trust badges; free in-home consultation offer.

### 7. Footprints Floors — the cleanest template
- **Site:** https://footprintsfloors.com/jacksonville/.../fernandina-beach — national-franchise polish: a **"Cities We Serve" directory**, per-service subpages, testimonials, strong free-estimate CTAs. Best web/UX of the group.
- **Copy:** "Cities we serve" structure + per-service subpages.

- **Carpet Masters** (carpet-masters.com): cleaning-led, brands **"5-Star Rated"** on every page; one landing page per service+city. *Lesson: aggressive social-proof branding.*

## What your site already does well
You're ahead of most of this field on the fundamentals — worth knowing before adding anything:
- ✅ Quote form is already best-practice: 5 fields, single-purpose, **phone optional / email required**, a **Project Type dropdown**, friendly copy.
- ✅ **Sticky mobile CTA bar** (Call + Get a Quote) — the exact pattern shown to lift mobile conversions 12–27%.
- ✅ Click-to-call phone link, photo gallery, **NTCA Certified** badge, and a Service Area section listing your towns.
- ✅ Web3Forms (no backend), now on Cloudflare.

## Highest-impact additions (ranked)

1. **Build per-city / per-service landing pages.** This is *the* dominant winning pattern here — Sand & Stone, Noble Granite, Florida Mud Masters, Footprints all do it. Create pages like *"Tile Installation in Fernandina Beach," "Backsplash Installation Amelia Island," "Quartz Countertops Yulee," "Tile & Stone in Nassau County."* Biggest SEO lever you're currently missing. [gomarketing, handoff.ai, contractoraccelerator]

2. **Launch a review-generation habit + a live reviews widget.** Your rivals' single biggest asset is review volume (American Flooring ≈270, Noble ≈274) — yet your two *name-closest* tile rivals (Sand & Stone, Tri-City) have almost none. Tie a review request to every finished job and embed a live Google-reviews widget (Trustindex, like Florida Mud Masters). ~87% of customers avoid low-rated firms. [gomarketing, webfx]

3. **Add Review/AggregateRating + LocalBusiness + FAQ schema (JSON-LD).** Gets star ratings into search results and feeds AI answer boxes; reported CTR gains of 25%+. Add `areaServed` for your towns. [eseospace, brightlocal]

4. **Publish a price range** (Sand & Stone's $8–$25/sq ft). Transparency is rare here and builds trust — even a "typical project starts at $X/sq ft" band differentiates you.

5. **Add before/after project pages.** Not just a grid — each with overview, the problem solved, timeline, and a customer quote. The "I want that" transformation effect is the top conversion content for remodelers. [builderfunnel, webfx]

6. **Add an FAQ page and a stone care / repair & restoration service line.** FAQ feeds schema + AI overviews; restoration (Marble Masters' model) creates repeat revenue and reasons to re-contact past clients.

7. **Pursue the Amelia Island custom-builder channel** (Ashian's niche) with a "For Builders & Designers" page — and you'll already beat them on a modern, mobile-first site.

8. **Protect page speed / Core Web Vitals.** 47% of users expect <2s loads; only ~42% of mobile sites pass all three CWV — passing is an edge. Keep hero images optimized. [harunstudio, digitalapplied]

9. **Lean into local identity** the way Tri-City does ("family-owned, on the island") and add a second top-of-funnel offer (e.g., a downloadable "Tile & Stone Material Guide") to capture browsers who aren't ready to request a quote yet.
